# Advanced Directive Concept Inventory

## Overview
This inventory catalogs 50 advanced directive templates across multiple categories for building a comprehensive concept inventory. Templates are organized by type, jurisdiction, and specialized population.

---

## Category 1: State-Specific Advance Directives (US)

### 1. California Advance Health Care Directive
- **Source**: California Probate Code Section 4701
- **URL**: https://www.caringinfo.org/planning/advance-directives/by-state/california/
- **Key Features**: Power of Attorney for Health Care, Living Will, Organ Donation, Primary Physician Designation
- **Witness Requirements**: Two witnesses OR notary public
- **Special Provisions**: Skilled nursing facility patients require patient advocate/ombudsman witness

### 2. Texas Directive to Physicians and Family or Surrogates
- **Source**: Texas Health & Safety Code Chapter 166
- **URL**: https://www.hhs.texas.gov/regulations/forms/advance-directives
- **Key Features**: Statutory form, separate from Medical Power of Attorney
- **Note**: Texas requires statutory form for POLST

### 3. New York Health Care Proxy
- **Source**: New York Public Health Law Article 29-C
- **URL**: https://www.health.ny.gov/forms/doh-1430.pdf
- **Key Features**: Agent appointment focus, separate living will recommended
- **Witness Requirements**: Two witnesses required

### 4. Florida Advance Directive
- **Source**: Florida Statutes Chapter 765
- **URL**: https://www.floridahealthfinder.gov/reports-guides/advance-directives.aspx
- **Key Features**: Designation of Health Care Surrogate, Living Will
- **Witness Requirements**: Two witnesses required

### 5. Pennsylvania Advance Directive
- **Source**: 20 Pa.C.S. Chapter 54
- **URL**: https://www.aging.pa.gov
- **Key Features**: Combined Health Care Power of Attorney and Living Will

### 6. Illinois Statutory Short Form Power of Attorney for Health Care
- **Source**: 755 ILCS 45
- **URL**: https://www.illinois.gov/services/health/advance-directives
- **Key Features**: Comprehensive statutory form

### 7. Ohio Living Will Declaration
- **Source**: Ohio Revised Code Chapter 2133
- **URL**: https://www.aging.ohio.gov
- **Key Features**: Separate Living Will and Health Care Power of Attorney forms

### 8. Michigan Patient Advocate Designation
- **Source**: Michigan Compiled Laws 700.5506-5515
- **URL**: https://www.michigan.gov/mdhhs
- **Key Features**: Patient advocate vs. general durable power of attorney distinction

### 9. Georgia Advance Directive for Health Care
- **Source**: O.C.G.A. § 31-32
- **URL**: https://aging.georgia.gov
- **Key Features**: Statutory form with living will and healthcare agent sections

### 10. North Carolina Advance Directive
- **Source**: N.C.G.S. Chapter 32A
- **URL**: https://www.ncdhhs.gov
- **Key Features**: Health Care Power of Attorney and Living Will combination

## Category 3: Specialized Population Templates

### 17. Five Wishes Advance Directive
- **Source**: Aging with Dignity
- **URL**: https://www.fivewishes.org/five-wishes-sample.pdf
- **Key Features**: User-friendly language, addresses comfort and spiritual needs
- **Legal Status**: Valid in 46 states + DC without attachment
- **Languages**: Available in 30+ languages
- **The Five Wishes**:
  1. Person to make care decisions
  2. Medical treatment preferences
  3. Comfort preferences
  4. Treatment by others
  5. What loved ones should know

### 18. Voicing My Choices (Adolescent/Young Adult)
- **Source**: National Cancer Institute / NIH
- **URL**: https://ccr.cancer.gov/pediatric-psycho-oncology/ppop-advance-directives
- **Key Features**: Designed for young people with serious illness
- **Target Population**: Adolescents and young adults

### 19. Dementia-Specific Advance Directive
- **Source**: Dementia-Directive.org (Dr. Barak Gaster)
- **URL**: https://dementia-directive.org
- **Key Features**: Stage-specific preferences, Alzheimer's-focused
- **Endorsement**: Featured in New York Times

## Key Concepts Across Templates

### Legal Components
1. **Healthcare Proxy/Agent Designation** - Naming decision-maker
2. **Living Will** - Treatment preferences documentation
3. **Durable Power of Attorney** - Legal authority transfer
4. **Witness Requirements** - Varies by state (0-2 witnesses, notary options)
5. **Capacity Requirements** - Mental competence at signing

### Common Decision Points
1. CPR/Resuscitation preferences
2. Mechanical ventilation
3. Artificial nutrition and hydration
4. Dialysis
5. Antibiotics for life-threatening infections
6. Pain management priorities
7. Organ/tissue donation
8. Autopsy consent
9. Disposition of remains

### Population-Specific Considerations
1. **Dementia**: Stage-specific preferences, cognitive decline planning
2. **Mental Health**: Medication preferences, ECT, restraint/seclusion
3. **Pediatric**: Parental authority, advance care plans vs. directives
4. **Religious**: Faith-tradition specific language and authority structures

### Activation Triggers
1. Incapacity determination by physician
2. Terminal condition diagnosis
3. Permanent unconsciousness
4. Specific disease progression stages
